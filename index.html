Gebra School

Gebra school is located at MESERANI, an area outside of Arusha, Tanzania. The Maasai children who live in this area rarely have the opportunity to obtain a formal education.

The Tilya family saw this need in their community and started a nursery school in 2001. Their first building was a mud and wooden house built by their own hands. Within a few years, they had almost 200 students, ages 4 to 7. Because the parents of the students had limited resources, the school provided for medical care, stationary, food, and clothes.

In 2008, the founder and manager of the school – Gebra Tilya – passed away. For several years, the school did not operate. Then, in January 2016, Gebra's son Gerald partnered with another family to restart the school. They opened their doors as the GEBRA MEMORIAL SCHOOL, in honor of Gerald's late fater. The school now has more than 45 students.
